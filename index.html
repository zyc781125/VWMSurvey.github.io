Abstract

The ability to understand, simulate, and interact with the physical world through visual observation is fundamental to Artificial General Intelligence. Visual world model (VWM), a system that learn to simulate environmental dynamics directly from high dimensional visual streams, emerges as a critical paradigm bridging perception, prediction, and decision-making. However, this rapid evolution with communities in video generation, reinforcement learning, and predictive architecture develops distinct approaches and creates a confusing landscape of conflicting terminologies, non-standardized evaluations and redundant efforts, obscuring what counts as progress for the field. This survey provides the first systematic, technology-oriented analysis of VWM by explicitly addressing their visual nature as both a central challenge and defining characteristic. We establish a conceptual framework that decomposes VWM into three essential components: the perceptual foundation, the cognitive core and the key capability. We then present a comprehensive taxonomy of four main technical paradigms analyzed through this framework, and provide an extensive review of the evaluation ecosystem across diverse world modeling domains. By linking core concepts to technical implementations and evaluation ecosystem, this survey aims to advance VWM from specialized tools toward foundational components for embodied intelligence, ultimately enabling artificial systems to truly comprehend and interact with the world.
